So, if you haven't heard of the [Onion Architecture](http://jeffreypalermo.com/blog/the-onion-architecture-part-1/), you wouldn't be alone. However, it's been around for a while under the name of [Hexagonal Architecture](http://alistair.cockburn.us/Hexagonal+architecture), due solely to a graphic created when [Alistair Cockburn](http://alistair.cockburn.us/) first described it. It's also known as the [Ports & Adapters Pattern](http://c2.com/cgi/wiki?PortsAndAdaptersArchitecture). I highly recommend reading all of the links I've provided thus far however the [Onion Architecture](http://jeffreypalermo.com/blog/the-onion-architecture-part-1/) version of this pattern described by [Jeffrey Palermo](http://jeffreypalermo.com/) was the most understandable to me. I dug around for a while until I could find someone who implemented a sample project using the [Onion Architecture](http://jeffreypalermo.com/blog/the-onion-architecture-part-1/) and came across [Matt Hidinger's presentation](http://www.matthidinger.com/archive/2011/05/17/Onion-Architecture-code-and-slides-from-Chicago-Code-Camp.aspx) he did for the Chicago Code Camp last year. I destructed [his sample project](http://onionarch.codeplex.com/), documented it, and rebuilt it in a shape I felt I could teach and support. I then [Templified](http://opensource.endjin.com/templify/) it.  

  

[Here is that solution for Visual Studio 2010](https://github.com/grimlor/OnionArchitecture) on GitHub.  

[Here is the Templify template](https://github.com/grimlor/OnionArchitecture/raw/master/onion-architecture-templified-v1.1.pkg).
